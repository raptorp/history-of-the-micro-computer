MICROCOMPUTER 2.0:

The Personal Computer

The software operating system

A contender to the microcomputer was IBM's so-called Personal Computer or PC although it was much pricier and did not find its way into normal homes straight away.

Much began to change as the new IBM PC entered the market. The IBM PC used a software operating system. This had to load on startup but made it easier to update - simply because you did not need to buy a completely new piece of hardware. The OS was called MSDOS, this was short for Micro-Soft Disk Operating System (yes, they used a dash in their name in the early days). The code was based on the UNIX operating system although greatly simplified.

Toddlers and PC's

I can't remember a time where we didn't have a computer in my home - I can't even remember a time where I didn't have a computer/phone/gameboy on my person at all times, so I had to call up my mom to help.
My family got our first PC in 1997, when I was 3-years old. It was an IBM 340 - one of the first "budget" models - and it probably saved my mom from endless hours of handwriting assignments for uni.

My mom had the policy of always answering questions truthfully (no Tooth Fairy or Santa-lies here), so when 3-year-old me asked her what the big grey box on the desk was, she told me. That was a big mistake. Little did she know, that 3-year-old me had just discovered back-talk and that I was just about to develop a new unforeseen level of sass and attitude.

After surviving the inquisition of a toddler and being proud that she fulfilled her motherly duties and broadened my horizon, she thought everything would go on as normal. Oh boy was she wrong. Afterwards when she asked 3-year-old me where something was I would respond with phrases like:

I'll check the hard drive … hard drive is deleted.

Or when she needed me to do something, ie. get ready for kindergarten or put on pants:

I ask computer … computer says no. Not right now, am on screen saver. Try again later.

Kindergarten teachers have heard me tell other kids to turn TOY_NAME off and on again. when toys were broken.


The obvious takeaway from this is:

  1. My attitude-problem is a chronic condition
  2. Never give a toddler knowledge you don't want them to turn around and throw right back at you
